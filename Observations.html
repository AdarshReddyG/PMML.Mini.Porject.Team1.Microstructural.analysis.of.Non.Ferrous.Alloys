Cu Alloys :

Specimen preparation :

- Specimen preparation for Cu alloys is not particularly difficult.

- Cut the required part of alloy, Mount it in the mounting press and proceed for grinding followed by polishing.

- Removal os scratches of fine scratches in pure Cu and single phase Cu is difficult when compared to two phase alloys.

- Vibrating polishing is useful for Cu alloys also chemical or electrolytic polishing is commonly used

- For leaded Cu alloys, etching prior to fine diamond polishing make the process easy.

Al Alloys :

Specimen preparation :

- Cut the required part of alloy, Mount it in the mounting press and proceed for grinding followed by polishing.

- Polishing of high purity Al is difficult. Polishing of alloys and cold worked samples is simple and easy.

- Vibratory polishing is used for Al alloys.

- For Al alloys, after polishing, a thin, transparent oxide film forms which can make etching difficult.
